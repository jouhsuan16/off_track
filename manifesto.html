OFF TRACK

1 | Reality

This world prefers uniformity.
A single rhythm. A single goal. A single answer.
The difference is treated as noise.
Deviation is treated as an error.

2 | Refusal

We do not treat agreement as a virtue.
We do not manufacture consensus for comfort.
We reject the myth of one correct path to life.
There is no standard template.
There is no final version.

3 | Practice

We protect disagreement.
We allow thoughts to remain unfinished.
We allow relationships to hold tension.
We do not rush to resolve one another.

4 | Position

Disagreement is not failure.
Refusal is not destruction.
Multiple paths exist at once
is the space we choose to defend.

Our Rotating Roles

Responsible for safeguarding rhythm and structure:

  • Reminds the group to slow down
  • Ensures discussions are not overly compressed
  • Maintains boundaries and time

They do not control direction; they protect the process.

Responsible for protecting disagreement:

  • Calls attention to overlooked voices
  • Prevents premature consensus
  • Encourages unfinished ideas to remain present

They are not contrarians, but guardians of complexity.

Responsible for:

  • Recording discussions
  • Preserving unresolved questions
  • Tracking the evolution of collective decisions

They ensure we do not forget in the acceleration of progress.

Responsible for:

  • Noticing limits of capacity
  • Monitoring overextension
  • Supporting redistribution of tasks

They protect sustainability, not efficiency.

Responsible for:

  • External communication
  • Articulating the collective's position
  • Maintaining collaborative relationships

They do not speak as individuals, but translate the collective's agreements and disagreements.
